Red Velvet Cake (with cream cheese frosting)


The only Red Velvet Cake recipe you’ll ever need! It’s soft, moist, fluffy, rich, strikingly beautiful and decadently delicious.

A cake fit for any celebration and sure to impress!

Here are some ingredients to make this delicious and moist red velvet cake.

  • All-purpose flour
  • Unsweetened cocoa powder
  • Baking soda
  • Salt
  • Granulated sugar and powdered sugar
  • Unsalted butter
  • Vegetable oil
  • Eggs and egg yolk
  • Red food coloring
  • Buttermilk
  • White vinegar
  • Cream cheese

10 easy steps to make Red Velvet Cake from scratch

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees. Butter 3 9-inch round baking pans then line bottom of each with a round of parchment paper and butter parchment, set aside.

  2. Whisk dry ingredients: Add flour, baking soda and salt to a large mixing bowl.

  3. Sift in cocoa powder then whisk mixture 20 seconds, set aside.

  4. Cream fats and sugar: In the bowl of an electric stand mixer fitted with the paddle attachment, whip together sugar and butter until pale and fluffy (this will take several minutes whipping on high speed). Mix in vegetable oil. Scrape down bowl.

  5. Blend in eggs and color: Add in eggs one at a time, mixing just until combined after each addition. Mix in egg yolks then red food coloring. Scrape down bowl.

  6. Whisk wet ingredients: Mix together buttermilk, vanilla and vinegar (in liquid measuring cup is fine).

  7. Mix in dry and wet mixtures: Add in flour mixture in 3 additions alternating with 2 additions of buttermilk mixture, mixing just until combined after each addition.

  8. Fold batter:Scrape down sides and bottom of bowl and gently fold batter just to ensure it is evenly incorporated.

  9. Pour into pans: Divide mixture evenly among three prepared baking pans. Spread into an even layer.

  10. Bake just until set: Bake in preheated oven 25–30 minutes until toothpick inserted into center of cake comes out clean.

  11. Cool before frosting: Cool in pan 10 minutes then run knife around edges of cake to loosen and invert onto wire racks to cool completely.
